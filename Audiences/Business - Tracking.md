# Audience: Business Decision-Makers — Tracking

**Product:** Business subscription — climatenotebooks.net

---

## Who They Are

Professionals charged with tracking climate change for their organization: sustainability officers, ESG leads, risk managers, strategy staff, corporate counsel, investor relations teams. They need to stay current on climate science, policy, regulation, and business risk. They are currently doing this with a patchwork of reports, newsletters, and consultants.

They have probably never heard of NotebookLM.

---

## What They Want

- Reliable, current intelligence on climate risk and policy without hiring a full-time analyst
- Confidence that they are not missing something important
- Ability to answer specific questions quickly when leadership asks
- Credible sourcing they can cite internally

---

## The Core Pitch

*The Climate Web contains over 100,000 vetted sources on climate change, risk, and policy — 35 years of professional curation. The business subscription gives you on-demand access to that corpus through NotebookLM: ask any question, get an answer drawn from the most comprehensive climate intelligence resource in existence.*

---

## Messaging Notes

- Lead with the problem: staying current on climate is a real job, and existing tools are inadequate
- The 100,000-source corpus is the differentiator — name it specifically
- NotebookLM education is essential: position it as "an on-demand analyst that has read everything"
- Credibility matters more here than for individuals — the IPCC Nobel, WRI 1988, LSE textbook are relevant
- ROI framing: what does it cost to be wrong about climate risk vs. what does a subscription cost

---

## Channel Priorities

1. LinkedIn (Wednesday content posts, direct outreach)
2. Email (targeted campaigns)
3. Webinar: business track session
4. Direct org outreach

---

## Content That Works for This Audience

- "Why your company's climate risk assessment is probably wrong" — and how to fix it
- Specific regulatory/policy developments surfaced from the corpus
- The gap between what corporate climate reports say and what the science says
- NotebookLM demo: a real business question, answered in real time
- Case framing: what it costs to be behind on climate intelligence
