## Format Notes

- Keep posts to 150-250 words — LinkedIn rewards concision
- First line is the hook — it shows before "see more" cutoff, make it count
- No links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses reach) — put the link in the first comment
- End with a single clear CTA or question
- Use line breaks generously — walls of text don't get read

## Commenting Strategy

Commenting is a high-leverage outreach tool — it puts your expertise in front of someone else's audience, builds credibility, and surfaces interested contacts without requiring them to find you first.

**The rule:** lead with expertise, trail with the notebook when genuinely relevant. The comment must stand on its own as a valuable contribution. The notebook reference, if it appears at all, is a one-liner at the end: "We've built a notebook specifically on this — happy to share if useful." That's helpful, not promotional.

**Target post types — prioritize these:**

| Post type | Why | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Climate risk assessments or corporate commitments | High engagement, often overstated — you can add specific, credible pushback | Lead with the specific gap or error; notebook reference if directly relevant |
| Carbon markets / offsets | Your direct expertise; most commentary is shallow | Add the historical context or structural observation others can't |
| IPCC reports or climate science coverage | Misreadings are common; you can correct without being dismissive | Clarify the actual finding; notebook reference if it goes deeper |
| Policy developments (EPA, SEC climate disclosure, EU taxonomy) | Business audience pays attention here | Add the "what this actually means" layer |
| "We're committed to net zero by 2050" announcements | High visibility, often warrant scrutiny | Measured, specific observation — not a takedown, but not empty validation either |

**Post types to skip:**
- Posts where you'd just be agreeing
- Viral hot takes where the comment thread is noise
- Posts outside your expertise where the comment would be generic

**Cadence:** 3-5 substantive comments per week. Quality over volume — one comment that gets 20 replies is worth more than ten that get none.

**Notebook references work best when:**
- The post is asking a question the notebook directly answers
- The commenter or author engages and asks for more
- The topic maps cleanly to a specific notebook (not "our notebooks cover this generally")
