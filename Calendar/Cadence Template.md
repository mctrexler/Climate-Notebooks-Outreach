# Cadence Template

Recommended publishing rhythm for The Climatographers outreach. Designed for a three-person team (Mark, Laura, Marcus) starting from a standing stop. The principle is consistency over volume — a cadence you can sustain is worth more than an ambitious one you abandon.

---

## Recommended Weekly Rhythm

### LinkedIn — 3 posts/week (Mon / Wed / Fri)

LinkedIn is your highest-leverage channel: 16,000 contacts, professional audience that overlaps all three tracks, and organic reach that rewards consistency.

| Day       | Post Type                      | Notes                                                                                            |
| --------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Monday    | **NotebookLM Education**       | What it is, what it can do, why it matters for climate intelligence. This is your top-of-funnel. |
| Wednesday | **Content excerpt or insight** | A striking finding, chart, or argument from the notebooks. Show the product working.             |
| Friday    | **Engagement / Question**      | A prompt that invites response. Builds the relationship and surfaces who's paying attention.     |

Rotate audience framing week to week: one week pitched toward individuals, next toward business decision-makers.

### Facebook — 2 posts/week (Tue / Thu)

Repurpose and lightly adapt LinkedIn content. Facebook reaches a different slice of the concerned-individual audience. Don't create from scratch — adapt.

### Email Newsletter — Bi-weekly (every other Tuesday)

One newsletter every two weeks to the full list. Each issue: one featured insight from the notebooks, one NotebookLM education item, one call to action (subscribe, share, book a call). Keep it short — 300-400 words plus links.

---

## Monthly

### Webinar — 1 per month

The single most important activity in the early phase. People cannot want what they don't understand. Each webinar should do two things: (1) show what NotebookLM is and what it can do, (2) demonstrate it with climate content so the value is concrete.

Suggested format: 45 minutes — 20 min demo, 15 min Q&A, 10 min on the notebooks and subscription offer. Record every session and repurpose clips.

**Suggested webinar series to start:**
1. *What is NotebookLM and why does it change how you engage with climate intelligence?* — general audience
2. *Climate risk for business: how NotebookLM turns 100,000 sources into an on-demand analyst* — business track
3. *Beyond the report: building a custom climate knowledge system for your organization* — advisory track

### Direct Org Outreach — 5–10 targeted contacts/month

Warm, personalized email to sustainability officers, ESG leads, climate policy staff at companies and organizations. Not mass email — targeted. Track in [[Contacts & Outreach/Org Targets]].

---

## Quarterly

- Review metrics: what's getting engagement, what isn't
- Refresh the NotebookLM education series as the product evolves
- Major email campaign to full list (new notebook launch, webinar series, product update)
- Reassess cadence and adjust

---

## Content Sourcing

The notebooks are the product — they are also the content. Every post, newsletter, and webinar can draw directly from what's already been built. This is the structural advantage: you are not generating content from nothing, you are surfacing content that already exists.

The pipeline should feel like curation and framing, not original writing from scratch.
